Applying Derivatives to Aquaculture Optimization

Modeling the growth velocity of Genetically Improved Farmed Tilapia (GIFT) in Malaysia.

The Bioscience Scenario

In Malaysian aquaculture, harvesting fish at the exact onset of the plateau phase maximize yield while minimizing feed waste. Using structural data trends compiled by the FAO Aquaculture Profile and WorldFish Malaysia (Penang), we can model the average mass $f(x)$ of a GIFT Tilapia over a 24-week cycle using a Gompertz sigmoid function.

By calculating the first derivative $f'(x)$, food security analysts pinpoint the exact week where growth acceleration drops, indicating the point of diminishing returns for feed input.

Growth Function
$f(x) = 600 \cdot e^{-e^{-0.25(x - 10)}}$
Yields expected weight $W$ in grams at week $x$.
First Derivative (Rate of Change)
$f'(x) = 150 \cdot e^{-0.25(x - 10) - e^{-0.25(x - 10)}}$
Yields instantaneous growth velocity in grams per week.
